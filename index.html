Submission title: Impact of Climate Change on Alpine Flora

Author(s): Dr. Jane Williams, Dr. Rohan Mehta, Prof. Ana FernÃ¡ndez, Dr. Linh Tran, Dr. Marcus Allen

Submitted on: 15 Mar 2025

This study investigates the relationship between microbial diversity and soil carbon storage across different land-use patterns. Through multi-region DNA sequencing and geochemical modeling, we identify key microbial communities contributing to enhanced carbon retention, offering valuable insights for sustainable land management. Our results demonstrate a statistically significant increase in soil carbon levels in plots with higher microbial functional diversity. In addition, long-term monitoring reveals a correlation between specific taxa abundance and seasonal carbon flux stability. These findings support the hypothesis that microbial ecosystem services play a critical role in climate change mitigation. By integrating microbiome data with environmental variables, this work presents a scalable framework for predicting soil carbon dynamics across ecological gradients.
